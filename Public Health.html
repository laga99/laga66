Public Health


LAGA66 | The definition of public health science according to Professor Winslow of Yale University (Leavel and Clark, 1958) is the science and art of preventing illness, prolonging life, improving physical and mental health, and efficiency through organized community efforts to improve environmental sanitation, infection control in the community, Individual education on personal hygiene, organizing medical services and care, for early diagnosis, disease prevention and social development, which will support everyone in the community to have a strong standard of living to maintain their health.

The American Doctor Association, AMA, (1948) defines Public Health as the science and art of preserving, protecting and promoting public health through community organizing efforts.


Scope of Public Health

1.Epidemiology is the study of health patterns and diseases and related fakors at the population level. This is a public health research corestone model, and helps inform evidence-based medicine to identify disease risk factors and determine optimal treatment approaches for clinical practice and for preventive medicine.

2.Biostatistics
Biostatistics is a statistical method applied to health-related sciences, such as medicine and public health, to help understand the characteristics of the population, and the relationship / influence of variables on the population

3.Environmental Health
Environmental Health is a subset of the basics of modern public health covering all aspects of human beings in relation to the environment, linked in ecosystems, with the aim of improving and maintaining human health values at the highest level, by not modifying Only social factors and the physical environment alone, but also all the attributes and behavior of the environment that can have an effect on the welfare, health and safety of mankind

4.Health and Behavior Education
Health and Behavior Education is Health education to support other health programs .. Health education is not directly bring benefits to the community and that is easily seen or measured, because education is a long-term behavioral investment.

5.Public Health Administration
Public Health Administration is a process of planning, organizing, directing, supervising, coordinating and assessing the available Resources, Procedures and Abilities to meet the needs and demands of health, medical care and a healthy environment by providing and organizing individual health efforts , Groups and communities.

6.Community Nutrition
Understanding Nutrition in general is a process of organisms using food consumed normally through the process of digestion, absorption, transportation, storage, metabolism and expenditure of substances that are not used to maintain the normal life, growth and function of the organs, and generate energy.

7.Occupational Health and Safety
Occupational Health and Safety (K3) is a field related to the health, safety and welfare of people working in an institution or project site.

8.Community Reproductive Health
Health Reproduction of society is a state of complete physical, mental and social prosperity in all matters relating to the function, role and reproductive system of society.

9.Health Information System
Health information system is an information management throughout all levels of government systematically in order to service the community.
