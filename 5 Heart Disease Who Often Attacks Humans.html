5 Heart Disease Who Often Attacks Humans




1. Heart Failure 
Heart Failure is a state where the heart can not provide blood flow to meet the needs of the body and potentially deadly. Many people assume that heart failure is defined as a heart attack, but it is not a heart attack.

Symptoms of heart failure include the following:
  • Swelling of the feet and hands
  • Add or lose weight before swelling due to excess fluid
  • Often shortness of breath
  • Often feels weak


2. Heart Valve Disease 
Heart Valve Disease is a condition in which one or more heart valves do not work well.

Common causes of heart valve abnormalities include:
  • Infection
  • Age factor
  • And also other diseases.

Virtually no symptoms are found by people with heart valve abnormalities.

3. Arrhythmias 
Arithms are a type of heart disease that interferes with rhythm disturbances can be called the heartbeat. Heartbeats can be faster, slower, and irregular. The cause of this disease is the lack of calcium in the body and the occurrence of heart blood vessel blockage.

4. Pericarditis 
For heart disease this one is inflammation of the heart sac or pericardium resulting in fluid accumulation and thickening. This inflammation is caused due to viral infection and radiation therapy for breast cancer.

5. Coronary Heart Disease 
This heart disease is caused by a layer of fat or cholesterol in the wall of the pulse that clog the blood vessels. And the result of blockage by layers of fat and cholesterol is the disruption of blood supply process to and from the heart.
